I'm a computational biologist focussed on analysis, modeling, and simulation of b-cell repertoire dynamics in response to infection and vaccination. Current research directions include:

  • receptor sequence bioinformatics
  • somatic hypermutation profiles
  • stacked generalization
Before joining the Hutch, I worked with Brenton Graveley's RNA biology / alternative-splicing lab at UConn Health Ctr, contributing to the modENCODE and ENCODE projects. I received my PhD in computer science from UMass Amherst, specializing in Reinforcement Learning theory and optimal adaptive control (Andy Barto, PI) and held postdoctoral fellowships at the Gatsby Computational Neuroscience Unit, University College London and the Institute for Genomics and Bioinformatics at UC Irvine.
