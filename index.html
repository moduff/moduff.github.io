I'm a computational biologist focussed on analysis, modeling, and simulation of b-cell repertoire dynamics in response to infection and vaccination. Current research directions:

  • receptor sequence bioinformatics
  • somatic hypermutation models
  • agent-based simulation of germinal centers
  • elicitation of broadly-neutralizing antibodies
Prior to joining the Hutch in December 2018, I was a research assistant professor embedded with Brenton Graveley's RNA biology lab at UConn Health Ctr. I received my PhD in computer science from UMass Amherst in 2002, specializing in Reinforcement Learning theory, adaptive control, stochastic processes, and optimization theory (Andy Barto, PI), and I held postdoctoral appointments at the Gatsby Computational Neuroscience Unit in London UK and the Institute for Genomics and Bioinformatics at UC Irvine.
